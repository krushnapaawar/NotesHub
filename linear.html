When we think two linear equation in two variable at the same time they are called simultaneous equation.

Ex. (1) Solve the following simultaneous equations.

(1) 5x - 3y = 8 ; 3x + y = 2

Solution: Method I

5x - 3y = 8 . . . . . (I)

3x + y = 2 . . . . . (II)

Multiplying both sides of equation (II) by 3.

9x + 3y = 6 . . . . . (III)

5x - 3y = 8 . . . . . (I)

Now let us add equations (I) and (III)

   5x - 3y = 8
+  9x + 3y = 6
  ____________
  14x      = 14

∴ x = 1

Substituting x = 1 in equation (II)

3x + y = 2

3 × 1 + y = 2

3 + y = 2

∴ y = -1

Solution is x = 1, y = -1; it is also written as (x, y) = (1, -1)

4. Cramer's Rule

Cramer's Rule se equations solve karna bohot aasan hota hai. Isme hume 3 determinants nikalne hote hain: D, Dx, aur Dy.

Formulas:

x = Dx / D

y = Dy / D


Ex. (2) Solve using Cramer's Rule:

3x - 4y = 10
4x + 3y = 5

Solution:

Here, equations are already in standard form.

Step 1: Find D

3   -4
4     3
= (3 × 3) - (-4 × 4) = 9 + 16 = 25

Step 2: Find Dx

10   -4
5     3
= (10 × 3) - (-4 × 5) = 30 + 20 = 50

Step 3: Find Dy

3   10
4     5
= (3 × 5) - (10 × 4) = 15 - 40 = -25

Step 4: Find x and y

x = Dx / D = 50 / 25 ⇒ x = 2

y = Dy / D = -25 / 25 ⇒ y = -1

Final Answer: (x, y) = (2, -1)
