Evolution

1. Evolution is defined as gradual changes occuring in living organisms over a long duration. 2. It is a slow-going process through which the development of organisms is achived. 3. Evolution is thus the formation of new species due to changes in specific character of living organisms. 4. Changes in these specific character get accumulated over several generation of living organisms in resposne to natural selection.

Evidence of Evolution

Morphological Evidence

Morphological Evidence is based on the similarity of shape,size or structure of organs among a group of organisms proving that they evolved from same ancester.

Anatomical Evidenc

Anatomical Evidence is the evidence of evolution based on the similiraties in the Anatomical strucutre of bones and joints in the organ of animals.

Vestigal Organs

Degenerated or underdeveloped useless organ nof organisms are called as vestigal organs.

Connecting link are some plants and animals that shows morphological character by which they can be releted to two different groups of organisms.

Lamarckism

Lamarckism or the theory of inheritance of acquired characters was given by Jean-Baptiste Lamarck. ii. It states that the morphological changes occurring in living organisms are responsible for evolution. This concept was based on the principle of use and disuse of organs. iii. Morphological changes may occur gradually, either due to specific activities or laziness of that organism. iv. For e.g., browsing on leaves of tall plants caused the neck of the giraffe to become long; frequent hammering movements cause the shoulders of ironsmith to become strong; inactivity caused weakening of the wings of birds like emu; legs of swans and ducks became useful for swimming due to their inhabitation in water; snakes lost their legs due to burrowing habits; etc. v. Such ‘acquired characters’ are transferred from one generation to another. This is called as the theory of inheritance of acquired characters
